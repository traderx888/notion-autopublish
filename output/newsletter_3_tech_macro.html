AI's $3 Trillion Build-Out Spurs Debt Boom and Creates New Risk

Tech giants are funding massive data centre capex through debt markets — $200 billion borrowed in 2025 alone, with $250-300 billion projected for 2026. JPMorgan estimates total AI buildout at $5 trillion including power supply. Off-balance-sheet SPVs like Meta's $30 billion "Beignet" Louisiana data centre deal are a new credit innovation.

BIS has warned: higher leverage could amplify shocks to financial intermediaries. Only 35% of global cloud infrastructure is currently optimised for AI workloads.

Risk Alert
Fixed-income portfolios increasingly AI-heavy. Bond-equity correlation shifting higher. If AI monetisation is slower than expected, refinancing at higher spreads becomes costly. Technology tail risk is significant and underpriced.
Pattern-Based, Heavy-Language Apps at High Risk

Anthropic's Cowork product poses a direct threat to pattern-based application companies. The AI platform layer is consolidating, compressing margins for middleware and wrapper companies. Advanced AI chip market projected to grow from $116B (2024) to $600B (2033) per Bloomberg Intelligence.

Investment Implication
AI infrastructure (chips, power, data centres) remains the durable investment theme. Application layer faces margin compression from platform providers (Anthropic, OpenAI). Separate "picks and shovels" from "gold miners" in AI allocation.
Infleqtion's Public Debut Tests Quantum Computing Frenzy

Quantum computing IPOs will test whether the hype has substance. Infleqtion's listing is a bellwether for the broader quantum computing sector's readiness for public market scrutiny and revenue expectations.

FED Fed Chair Warsh & Institutional Constraints

What Could Warsh Do Unilaterally as Fed Chair?
如果沃什擔任聯儲會主席 他可以單方面採取哪些行動？

Fed Chair nominee Kevin Warsh's planned reforms (balance sheet reduction, FOMC restructuring, reduced policy communication) are constrained by consensus-driven institutional structure. FOMC (12 members) sets rates by majority vote; Board (7 members) controls balance sheet and regulation. Warsh has limited unilateral power.

Precedent: successful chairs (Volcker, Greenspan, Bernanke) built consensus. Failed chairs (G. William Miller, 18 months) did not. Presidential appointment window guarantees only 1 additional Board seat when Powell's term ends Jan 2028.

Investment Implication
Warsh's ability to implement reforms depends on persuasion, not legal authority. Near-term (2026-2027) policy likely reflects compromise between Warsh and incumbent governors. Unilateral balance sheet reduction unlikely. Market should not overreact to Warsh headlines — institutional inertia is strong.
