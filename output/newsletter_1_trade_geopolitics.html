Who Can Win From Tariff Refund Windfall?

The US Supreme Court's 6-3 ruling declared Trump's emergency tariffs unlawful, opening the door to billions in potential refunds. Hardest-hit countries (China, India, Japan, Germany, Korea) are biggest winners. Industrials mentioned tariffs 38.8% of the time in S&P 500 earnings transcripts. Nike warned of a $1 billion tariff hit.

Investment Implication
Near-term sector rotation favours machinery, automotive, and consumer discretionary (Nike, retailers) that took heavy duty hits. However, refund process shifts to Court of International Trade with potential delays.
Allies Lose, Foes Win as Trump Tariffs Reset
美國新關稅效果主次不分 盟友獲益反不及競爭對手

Paradoxical outcome: former targets (Brazil, China, India, South Africa) see major tariff reductions, while US allies (UK, EU, Japan) see minimal benefit. Brazil's rate drops from ~31% to ~10%, while Australia and UK remain locked at 10%.

Brazil: 31% → 10% (↓21pp)  |  China: 31% → 21% (↓10pp)  |  India: 20% → 11% (↓9pp)  |  UK/AU: no change
Investment Implication
LONG Brazilian exporters (autos, agriculture, minerals) and Indian manufacturers (EVs, tech). REDUCE UK/EU-dependent supply chains. Trade consensus breaking down; bilateral deal-making now favours non-allies.
China Exports Resilient, But Game Theory Has Changed
關稅下調固然是利好 但中國面臨的博弈格局已變

Shipping and port data show robust export activity: ~6% YoY growth estimated for January 2026. US share of China's exports fell to a record low of 11% (down from 19% peak). But tariff relief is temporary — China's $1.2 trillion trade surplus is causing backlash, and the real challenge is structural: weak domestic demand, not tariffs.

Investment Implication
Avoid betting on sustained tariff relief. The real bull case for China requires domestic demand stimulus (fiscal/monetary) — favouring consumer discretionary, services, and property plays. Supply chain diversification into ASEAN, India, Brazil, Mexico remains durable.
Xi's "Powerful Yuan" Vow — FX Appreciation This Time
強大的貨幣需要更自由的流動 中國經濟學家呼籲進一步開放資本項目

Yuan appreciation is being carefully managed by PBOC rather than resisted. Bloomberg Dollar Spot Index appreciated in Q1 in 5 of 7 years since 2019, but structural de-dollarization could trigger reversal. Yuan and MYR may hold higher FX-portfolio values than peers amid dollar uncertainty.

Investment Implication
Position for yuan/MYR strength as structural tailwinds build. Use near-term dollar strength for better entry. Monitor Fed independence rhetoric and US debt sustainability as catalysts for dollar reversal.

If You Can't Follow Iran, At Least Watch Oil

John Authers: The world remains strangely calm about escalating US-Iran tensions despite Trump's assertion the US is "ready, willing, and able." Prediction markets put US attack odds at 70%, yet broader markets continue to ignore the risk while Brent crude surged 4.3% above $70.

Intensive Diplomacy May Be Delaying Tactic
密集外交疑為緩兵之計 對伊朗軍事打擊可能性依然存在

Negotiations are tactical delay, not genuine compromise. Trump's demands (complete nuclear dismantling, missile restrictions, proxy cessation) far exceed Tehran's redlines. Time favours Washington: ability to strengthen military posture while considering aggressive measures like oil export blockades.

Will US Strike on Iran Cause Oil to Spike? Not Necessarily
美國對伊朗發動軍事打擊會導致油價飆升嗎？未必

Counterintuitive finding: of 7 distinct post-strike scenarios mapped, 4 result in no sustained oil spike. Oil impact depends on whether Iran survives initial strikes, whether retaliation is substantive vs. symbolic, and whether energy infrastructure is targeted. Only Hormuz Strait blockage triggers maximum impact.

Investment Implication
Oil call skew is potentially overpriced given the "strike won't spike" analysis. Tactical oil hedge can be sized down if Iran's response avoids energy infrastructure (50%+ probability). Equity risk appetite can separate from oil if strike doesn't disrupt supplies.
Gold Forecast Raised on Sticky Hedging Demand

Bloomberg's precious metals commentary raises gold price forecasts on persistent hedging demand. Geopolitical uncertainty and inflation persistence keep structural demand elevated.

Investment Implication
Gold remains a core hedge in portfolios. Hedging demand is structural, not cyclical — price floors have moved higher.

EUROPE ENERGY European Energy Security & Middle East Fallout

Break from Russia Won't Solve Energy Troubles

EU successfully severed Russian gas dependence (>50% pre-2022 → ~15% in 2025 after Ukraine transit shutdown), but benchmark gas prices remain 2x pre-war levels. US LNG share of EU imports surged from 14% (2021) to 57% (2025), creating new policy risk under Trump.

Russia gas share: 50% → 15%  |  US LNG share: 14% → 57%  |  Gas prices: 2x pre-war average
Investment Implication
Energy-intensive European exporters (chemicals, metals) face permanent competitive disadvantage vs. US. Grid infrastructure and renewables investment accelerating — capital allocation opportunity in European green tech.
How Middle East War Might Hit European Economy & Politics

Short war = contained damage. Prolonged war = oil above $100/bbl triggers +1.0pp inflation peak and -0.6% real GDP. ECB unlikely to cut aggressively despite growth damage — inflation memory constrains policy. Political risks: far-right gains in France (2027 presidential), fiscal deficits widen in Italy/France.

Investment Implication
Stagflation risk acute if war exceeds 3-6 months. Watch euro stability and sovereign spreads. ECB trapped between growth concerns and inflation memory.
Key Takeaways for Fund Managers
  • Tariff Rebalancing: Supreme Court ruling compresses tariff spread (10-12% vs. previous 13-31%). Winners are "enemies" not "allies" — Brazil, India, South Africa gain most. Refund windfall benefits machinery, autos, consumer discretionary.
  • China: Export resilience is real but tariff relief is temporary. Real bull case requires domestic demand stimulus. Yuan appreciation thesis valid if de-dollarization resumes.
  • Iran/Oil: 70% strike probability but oil impact is conditional. 4 of 7 scenarios show no sustained spike. Size oil hedges accordingly.
  • Gold: Core hedge position. Sticky hedging demand creates structural price floor.
  • Europe: Energy costs locked at 2x pre-war. US LNG leverage is new geopolitical risk. Stagflation scenario if Middle East war persists.
