Three Fixes to Make Takaichi's Fiscal Bet Pay Off

PM Sanae Takaichi's fiscal expansion strategy is sound, but success requires three critical reforms: (1) deploy AI to fill labour gaps (¥1 trillion allocated), (2) grant BOJ more independence from government pressure, (3) relax rigid employment regulations that inhibit innovation.

Investment Implication
Fiscal expansion without structural reform risks capital flowing to interest-sensitive sectors (real estate) rather than productivity-enhancing industries. Success requires coordinated AI investment, monetary independence, and labour liberalisation.
Takaichi BOJ Board Picks Signal Dovish Tilt
高市早苗提名人選預示日本央行政策或趨向鴿派

Two dovish economists (Toichiro Asada, Ayano Sato) nominated to the 9-member BOJ policy board. BOJ expected to move cautiously, with next rate hike likely in July, not before. Ruling coalition lacks upper house majority, requiring opposition support.

Investment Implication
Softer BOJ stance supports weak yen and keeps real rates negative longer. The yen carry trade has extended runway, but watch for opposition blocking nominees on cost-of-living concerns.
Tokyo CPI Softened by Subsidies — But Underlying Inflation Sticky
東京CPI因補貼效應走軟意味央行可放緩加息步伐

February Tokyo CPI softened to 1.6% (subsidies reducing headline by 0.25pp). Core CPI slowed from 2.0% to 1.8%. But core-core (ex-food & energy) accelerated to 2.5%, signalling underlying wage-price spiral persists.

Investment Implication
Subsidies mask inflation reality. When they expire, repricing may force faster hikes. BOJ likely pauses until July — but sticky core-core means the hiking cycle isn't over, just delayed.

S&P 500 Slumps: Weaker Growth, Stubborn Inflation

US equities caught between slowing growth (Q4 GDP 1.4% vs. ~2% expected) and sticky inflation (core PCE 0.4% in December, highest in nearly a year). Tech stocks down 4% YTD while energy/materials rallied 15%+. Active managers outperforming at highest rate since 2007.

Investment Implication
Stagflationary backdrop favours stock-pickers over index exposure. Rotation away from mega-cap Tech into cyclicals (energy, materials) suggests value revival — but risks reverse if growth concerns intensify.
Equity Rotation Lifts Dispersion, But Value Is Harder to Find

Rotation from broad beta to stock-specific moves has pushed realised dispersion to historical highs. SPX realised correlation sits in the 6th percentile (10-year history). Single-stock implied volatility elevated mid-30s vs. pre-COVID mid-20s. However, derivatives markets already price this — making dispersion trades richly valued.

Strategists Say European Markets Already Peaked

Stoxx Europe 600 expected to end 2026 flat from ~630 all-time high despite 5% YTD gains. Valuation at 16x forward earnings (20-year average: 13.3x) with demanding double-digit earnings growth expectations. 8 consecutive months of gains; EPS revisions negative for internationally-exposed stocks.

Investment Implication
European equities near peak with limited upside. Prefer domestic-oriented over international stocks. Heavy fiscal support and low rates provide floor, but earnings compression risk if growth disappoints.
FTSE 100 Offers Haven Appeal in Troubled Times

FTSE 100 outperformed peers during Iran escalation: commodities exposure (18%), defensives (health + staples 30%), aerospace/defence (7.4%). 75% of revenue earned outside UK (28% North America) benefits from weak pound. Valuation at 14.5x — compelling vs. US (21.3x) and Japan (23x). Fund managers net 15% underweight (down from 24%).

Investment Implication
FTSE 100 is the geopolitical hedge in equity markets. Weak pound tailwind, AI-shielded "HALO" names, and pension forced-selling completion create upside. Best risk/reward among major indices at current valuations.
SpaceX Mega IPO Reshapes 2026 Listing Landscape

SpaceX's potential mega IPO is reshaping the 2026 listing calendar, absorbing liquidity and setting valuation benchmarks for tech/space sector. DraftKings' prediction-market risk may be overstated at current low valuations. UBS carries hidden Credit Suisse tail risk the market may be underpricing.

Key Takeaways for Fund Managers
  • Japan: Dovish BOJ tilt extends yen carry trade runway. Next hike delayed to July. But core-core CPI at 2.5% means the hiking cycle isn't dead — just postponed. Monitor subsidy expiry timing.
  • US Equities: Stagflationary signal (weak GDP + sticky PCE). Rotation from Tech into commodities/defensives. 60% of active managers beating benchmarks — best environment for stock-picking since 2007.
  • Dispersion: At historical highs but already priced in derivatives. Long dispersion crowded. Vulnerable to macro-driven correlation spike.
  • Europe: Peaked at 16x. Limited upside. Prefer domestic over export-dependent names.
  • UK/FTSE: Best risk/reward at 14.5x. Geopolitical hedge, weak pound tailwind, defensive sector mix. Underweight positioning still being unwound.
  • UBS: Hidden Credit Suisse tail risk likely underpriced by the market.
